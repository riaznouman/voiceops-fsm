# Manager Persona

## Name
Rachel Torres

## Role
Operations Manager / Office Admin

## Age
35

## Background
Rachel manages a team of 8 field technicians. She handles scheduling, dispatching, customer communication, and reporting. She works from the office on a desktop computer and needs to keep track of where technicians are and how jobs are progressing throughout the day. She's used spreadsheets and basic scheduling tools before but wants something more organized.

## Goals
- See a real-time overview of all technicians and their current status
- Assign and reschedule work orders efficiently
- Monitor job progress without calling technicians
- Generate reports on completed jobs, response times, and technician performance
- Manage customer records and service history

## Frustrations
- Not knowing if a technician is running late until the customer calls
- Manually tracking job status on whiteboards or spreadsheets
- Double-booking technicians because the schedule isn't updated in real time
- Spending too much time on the phone coordinating instead of managing

## Device & Context
- Uses a desktop computer (Windows) with a large monitor
- Occasionally checks the web panel on her phone during off-hours
- Prefers data tables and list views over fancy dashboards
- Needs to handle multiple tasks at once — often has 3-4 browser tabs open

## Key Scenarios
1. Morning: Reviews the day's schedule, assigns any unassigned work orders
2. Midday: Checks job progress, handles customer calls about ETAs
3. Afternoon: Reassigns jobs if someone runs behind schedule
4. End of week: Reviews dockets and generates weekly reports
